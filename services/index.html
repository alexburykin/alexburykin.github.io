Action Plan & Estimate

01 Let’s Talk
How does it work?

In just one focused meeting, I will help you break down your concept into an actionable plan and provide a clear estimate. The goal is to get your project moving efficiently and with minimal friction.

What should you ask about it?

You should ask about timelines, deliverables, and any technical challenges you anticipate. It’s also helpful to discuss the scope of your idea to ensure everyone is on the same page.

The timeline depends on the complexity of your idea, but typically the action plan and estimate are ready within a week after the meeting.

How much involvement is required from you?

Your input is crucial during the initial planning stage to ensure the action plan reflects your vision accurately. After that, I can handle most of the heavy lifting.

What technologies will be used?

The technology stack will be chosen based on your project’s requirements. I prefer using scalable and maintainable technologies that fit your needs best.

How much will it cost?

An estimate will be provided based on the scope of the project and the resources needed to complete it. Transparency is key, and I ensure no hidden costs.

What will the deliverables include?

The deliverables will include a detailed action plan, timelines, and an estimate for executing the project.

Architecture Review
Analysis & Optimization

03 Let’s Talk
How does it work?

In a series of 2-4 meetings, we will evaluate your current architecture, identify bottlenecks, and map out improvements. This helps ensure your system can scale smoothly and handle increased demand.

How do you identify bottlenecks?

Bottlenecks are identified by analyzing the performance, scalability, and reliability of your system. We use metrics, logs, and code reviews to spot issues.

What tools are used in the review?

We use various profiling tools, logging, and monitoring services such as Datadog and New Relic to provide detailed insights into system performance.

Can the architecture be updated incrementally?

Yes, architectural changes can often be phased in incrementally to minimize disruption while still improving the system.

How do you prioritize improvements?

Improvements are prioritized based on impact, feasibility, and the specific needs of your business. The goal is to address the most critical issues first.

What are common architecture pitfalls?

Common pitfalls include excessive complexity, technical debt, and poor scalability. We’ll address these and ensure your architecture can support your future growth.

How do you ensure scalability?

Scalability is ensured by evaluating your current infrastructure and making strategic adjustments that support growth without compromising performance.
